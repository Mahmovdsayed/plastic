Facts

Production and consumption

-Production and consumption: About 400 million tons of plastic are produced annually worldwide, and about a third of it is used only once.
-Since plastic was invented in the 1940s, more than 9.1 billion tons of it have been produced, most of which is still in the environment.

Environmental impact

-90% of marine waste consists of plastic, which directly threatens marine life.
-100,000 marine creatures such as turtles and dolphins die every year from plastic pollution.
-Plastic has been found at the deepest point in the oceans, the Mariana Trench, showing how widespread it is.

Decomposition time period

-Most plastics take 400-1000 years to fully decompose, which means that almost every piece of plastic ever produced is still out there.
-1 Plastic bags alone can take 10-20 years to decompose, while plastic bottles take 450 years or more.
-Plastic has been found at the deepest point in the oceans, the Mariana Trench, showing how widespread it is.

Impact on humans

-Plastic breaks down into tiny particles known as microplastics, which seep into our water and food. Studies suggest that humans consume about 50,000 plastic particles each year.
-Microplastic particles have been found in human blood, and may have unknown health effects.

Alternatives and solutions

-If all the plastic in the world was recycled, the equivalent of 3 million barrels of oil could be saved annually.
-Switching to alternatives like cloth bags and reusable bottles can significantly reduce plastic pollution.
